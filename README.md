# Enterprise-Financial-Performance-Suite-3-Statement-Analysis-
**📌 Overview**
Built this to answer a simple question what is really going on in the business behind the numbers and turned scattered data into a dashboard that actually makes sense at a glance.

**⚙️ How It Was Built**

The process started with a raw dataset in Excel that contained financial records across multiple years. The data wasn’t ready to use directly, so it needed structuring and cleaning before any analysis.

First, I organized the data into a consistent format and handled missing or messy values. Once the structure was clear, the dataset was imported into Power BI where the actual modeling began.

Relationships were created between different tables to reflect how financial statements connect with each other. After that, DAX was used to build key measures such as profit, margins, and performance indicators.

Finally, the dashboard was designed with a focus on clarity making sure the visuals tell a story rather than just display numbers.

**📊 What the Dashboard Shows**

The report brings together the three core areas of financial analysis:

Income performance through revenue, cost, and profit
Financial position through assets, liabilities, and equity
Cash movement through operating cash flow

It also highlights supporting metrics like receivables, payables, and efficiency indicators to give more depth to the analysis.

**📈 Key Takeaways**
Revenue and profit show a steady upward trend
Costs are being managed more efficiently over time
The business maintains a strong position with higher equity than liabilities
Cash flow from operations is consistently improving
